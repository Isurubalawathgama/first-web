Coffee, a beloved beverage worldwide, originates from the roasted seeds of the Coffea plant, primarily Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora (Robusta). Thriving in tropical climates, coffee is chiefly cultivated in countries like Brazil, Vietnam, and Ethiopia. Harvested cherries undergo dry or wet processing to extract the beans, which are then roasted to develop complex flavors. Coffee is brewed in various methods, including drip, espresso, French press, and cold brew, each influencing the taste. Culturally significant, coffeehouses serve as social and intellectual hubs, while economically, coffee is a crucial global commodity supporting millions of livelihoods. Health-wise, coffee offers benefits like improved alertness and antioxidants but can cause issues if consumed excessively. Coffee's diversity in varieties and preparation styles—from Italian espressos to Ethiopian ceremonies—reflects its profound global impact and cultural richness.
